About Me

I am currently a PhD candidate in hydrology at the Technical University of Munich and the German Aerospace Center (DLR). My research focuses on flood risk and socio-hydrology in the Anthropocene. I utilize earth observation (satellite imagery), climate , and socio-economic data, to assess flood impacts. My work particularly addresses inequalities in flood and climate risk, as well as issues of climate justice. Previously, I earned a diploma in robotics and mechatronics for medical applications at Sorbonne University. I also worked at the University of Cambridge on deep learning applied to tumor imaging. However, given the current global warming situation, I decided to change my career path and focus on global climate change.
